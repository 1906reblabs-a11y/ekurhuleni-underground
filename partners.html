Commercial Partnerships

Reach the Audience That Takes Ekurhuleni Seriously

Ekurhuleni Underground is read by the people who build, plan, finance, and operate the East Rand's economy. If your organisation belongs in that conversation, this is where to join it.

Why Partner
A Different Kind of Readership

Most publications that cover the East Rand reach a general audience. Ekurhuleni Underground does not. Our readers sought us out because they are professionally or intellectually invested in understanding how this region actually works — its supply chains, its informal economies, its infrastructure dependencies, its transition from a manufacturing past toward whatever comes next. That specificity of interest creates a readership that is, by definition, pre-qualified: you do not read a deep analysis of the Boksburg water network for casual entertainment.

Our audience includes municipal planners, property developers, logistics and supply chain professionals, economists and researchers, investment analysts with interests in the Gauteng industrial belt, and the business owners and operators who run the visible and invisible economies this publication covers. They are not passive consumers. They are decision-makers who return to Ekurhuleni Underground precisely because it tells them things that no other publication does.

We do not sell eyeballs. We offer access to attention that is already directed at the economy you operate in.

Partnering with Ekurhuleni Underground is not an advertising transaction. It is an alignment — a signal to a specific professional community that your organisation understands the region deeply enough to want to be associated with serious analysis of it. That signal travels further than a banner impression, and it lasts considerably longer.

Partnership Options
How to Partner
  • Most Prominent
    Presenting Partner Annual — Exclusive

    A single organisation holds the Presenting Partner position for the full publication year. Your name appears in the masthead of every issue alongside the Ekurhuleni Underground brand — a persistent, uncluttered association with the publication's full body of work. There is one Presenting Partner at any time. No other partner appears at this level.

    • Named credit in every issue masthead: "Published in partnership with [Organisation]"
    • Logo and one-line description on the Partners page of the publication website
    • One dedicated annual feature — a 300-word editorial note, written by the Ekurhuleni Underground team, introducing your organisation's work in the context of the East Rand economy (factual, not promotional)
    • Early access to each issue before publication
    • Annual briefing: a 60-minute conversation with the editorial team on the themes and directions the publication is developing for the coming year
  • Issue Partner Per Issue — Limited to One

    One organisation partners with a single issue of Ekurhuleni Underground. Your name appears on the issue's header and footer as the issue partner — a clean, visible association with a specific piece of analysis that is relevant to your sector or geography. No more than one Issue Partner per issue. No Issue Partnership will be sold for an issue whose editorial content directly concerns the partner's own operations or industry.

    • Named credit in the issue header and footer: "This issue supported by [Organisation]"
    • Logo and link on the publication website's issue archive listing for that issue (permanent)
    • One social media acknowledgement at publication
  • Research Partnership Project-Based — By Arrangement

    For organisations with a substantive interest in the economic geography of Ekurhuleni — municipalities, universities, development finance institutions, foundations — we offer a structured research partnership. This is not an advertising arrangement. It is a commission: you fund an extended piece of analysis on a theme of mutual interest, which Ekurhuleni Underground researches and publishes independently. The editorial process and framing remain entirely under our control. The resulting publication is attributed as an Ekurhuleni Underground research edition, supported by your organisation.

    • Extended research edition (2,500–4,000 words) on an agreed theme
    • Full editorial independence — the final framing and conclusions are ours
    • Named support credit across the edition and its web presence
    • PDF version formatted for distribution and presentation use
    • Briefing session with your team on findings before publication
  • Community Partner Annual — Multiple Available

    Designed for smaller organisations, civic bodies, and local businesses that want to be associated with the publication's work without the scale of a Presenting Partnership. Community Partners are acknowledged on the publication's Partners page with a brief description of their work. This tier is intended for organisations that are genuinely part of the Ekurhuleni economic ecosystem — businesses operating in the region, local professional associations, research organisations focused on the East Rand.

    • Named listing on the Partners page with one-paragraph description and website link
    • Annual acknowledgement in one issue's editorial notes
    • Subscription to the editorial team's quarterly theme planning summary
Editorial Boundaries
What Partnership Does Not Buy
  • No editorial influence

    Partners have no input into what topics we cover, how we frame our analysis, or what conclusions we reach. This is unconditional.

  • No sponsored content

    We do not produce articles, editorials, or analysis that are written to serve a partner's commercial or reputational interests. Every word in every issue reflects the editorial team's independent judgment.

  • No conflict of interest

    We will not enter a partnership arrangement with an organisation whose interests would create a conflict with our ability to cover a topic freely. If we are covering your industry or your operations in an issue, we will not sell that issue's partnership to you.

  • No volume discounts for prominence

    Partnership tiers are defined by their function, not by how much an organisation pays. The Presenting Partnership is a single, exclusive position. We will not create additional tiers or modify the editorial acknowledgement structure to accommodate larger financial commitments.

Get in Touch
Starting a Conversation

We review partnership enquiries on a rolling basis. We are selective about who we partner with — not because we are difficult, but because the value of the association depends on it being genuine. We are most interested in organisations that are substantively engaged with the economic geography of Ekurhuleni: as operators, investors, researchers, planners, or practitioners.

If you are unsure whether a partnership makes sense for your organisation, the easiest thing is to start a conversation. Tell us who you are, what you do, and why you think Ekurhuleni Underground's readership is the right audience for your work. We will respond honestly about whether we think there is a fit.
